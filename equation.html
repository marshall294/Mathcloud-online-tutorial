Equations — Year 8

Lesson notes, worked examples and 12 assessment questions — responsive and interactive.

Topic: Linear equations • Level: Year 8

Learning objectives

  • Understand what an equation is and the meaning of the equals sign (=).
  • Solve one-step and two-step linear equations.
  • Solve equations with brackets and simple fractions.
  • Check solutions by substituting back into the original equation.

Key ideas & symbols

= means equal to. Example: 5 + 3 = 8

Unknowns are usually written as x or y. Example: x + 7 = 13

Common symbols: + add, - subtract, × multiply, ÷ divide, √ square root, ± plus or minus.

To solve an equation we do the same operations to both sides to keep it balanced.

Step-by-step method (basic)

1) Simplify both sides (expand brackets, collect like terms).
2) Move terms with the unknown to one side (use + or -).
3) Move numbers to the other side.
4) Divide or multiply to find the unknown.
5) Check by substituting the value back into the original equation.

Worked examples (with steps)

Example 1 — One-step
Solve: x + 7 = 13
  1. Subtract 7 from both sides: x = 13 − 7
  2. So x = 6. Check: 6 + 7 = 13.
Example 2 — Two-step
Solve: 3x + 4 = 19
  1. Subtract 4 from both sides: 3x = 15
  2. Divide both sides by 3: x = 5
  3. Check: 3×5 + 4 = 15 + 4 = 19.
Example 3 — Brackets
Solve: 2(x + 3) = 14
  1. Expand: 2x + 6 = 14
  2. Subtract 6: 2x = 8
  3. Divide by 2: x = 4
Example 4 — Fractions
Solve: \( \tfrac{1}{2}x = 6 \)
  1. Multiply both sides by 2: x = 12
  2. Check: \tfrac{1}{2} \times 12 = 6

Hints for checking answers

  • Always substitute your solution back into the original equation.
  • If both sides are equal after substitution, the solution is correct.
  • For fraction answers, give the exact fraction where possible (e.g. 14/3) or a decimal to 2 dp.

Answer key (with steps)

1. x + 7 = 13 → x = 13 − 7 = 6
2. 4x = 20 → x = 20 ÷ 4 = 5
3. 3x + 4 = 19 → 3x = 15 → x = 5
4. 2x − 5 = 9 → 2x = 14 → x = 7
5. 5x + 3 = 2x + 15 → 3x = 12 → x = 4
6. 2(x + 3) = 14 → 2x + 6 = 14 → 2x = 8 → x = 4
7. (1/2)x = 6 → x = 6 × 2 = 12
8. x/3 + 2 = 7 → x/3 = 5 → x = 15
9. 0.5x + 1 = 4 → 0.5x = 3 → x = 3 ÷ 0.5 = 6
10. −3x + 2 = 11 → −3x = 9 → x = −3
11. 4(x − 1) = 2x + 10 → 4x − 4 = 2x + 10 → 2x = 14 → x = 7
12. (3x + 2)/2 = 8 → 3x + 2 = 16 → 3x = 14 → x = 14/3 (≈ 4.666...)
