Introduction: Tissues and Organs

In Biology, a tissue is a group of similar cells that work together to carry out a specific function. An organ is made up of different tissues working together to perform a complex function in the body. Understanding tissues and organs helps us appreciate how living things are structured and how they function effectively.

Applications of Tissues and Organs

- In humans, the heart (an organ) is made up of muscle tissue, nerve tissue, and blood vessels, all working together to pump blood.
- In plants, the leaf (an organ) contains tissues such as the epidermis, mesophyll, and xylem/phloem which support photosynthesis and transport.
- Medical science depends on the understanding of tissues and organs to treat diseases, perform surgeries, and develop transplants.
